Getting to know someone takes time — and the best relationships are built on genuine attention, not a checklist. LUCI's adaptive learning works the same way. Rather than asking you to fill out a lengthy profile or flip a dozen switches, LUCI simply pays attention as you use it. Over days and conversations, it starts to pick up on the rhythms of how you work, what you care about, and how you like to be spoken to. The result feels less like a configured tool and more like a companion who actually knows you — quietly, naturally, and always on your terms.

## What LUCI Learns

LUCI builds a picture of your preferences through gentle observation. None of this is surveillance — think of it as the same kind of awareness a good friend develops after spending time with you. The signals LUCI pays attention to include:

- **Topics you return to** — If you frequently bring up a project, a hobby, or a recurring concern, LUCI notes that it matters to you.
- **Time of day you're most active** — Whether you're an early riser who checks in at 7am or a night owl who reflects at 11pm, LUCI picks up on when you show up.
- **Communication tone** — LUCI notices whether you tend to write in full sentences or quick fragments, whether you lean formal or casual, and mirrors that back to you.
- **Frequently used tasks** — Repeated actions — like asking for a daily summary, drafting messages, or creating to-do lists — become part of LUCI's understanding of what you actually need.
- **Preferred response length** — If you consistently engage more with shorter replies, or regularly ask LUCI to elaborate, that pattern becomes a preference.

<Note>
These are inferences drawn from your behavior — not data points being tracked and stored in a dossier. LUCI is reading the conversation, not cataloguing you.
</Note>

## How Adaptation Shows Up

Adaptation is designed to feel like a natural shift, not a sudden change. You might not even notice it happening at first — and that's intentional. Here are some concrete ways LUCI's learning surfaces in your day-to-day experience:

- **Greetings that match your moment** — In the morning, LUCI might open with "Good morning — ready to get started?" By evening, it might say "Hope your day went well. Want to wind down or wrap something up?"
- **Proactive task suggestions** — If you've mentioned a weekly team meeting several times, LUCI might gently ask on Monday morning whether you'd like help preparing for it.
- **Response length calibration** — If you consistently prefer concise answers, LUCI stops offering lengthy explanations unless you ask. If you love detail, it leans in.
- **Tone matching** — A user who writes casually gets a more relaxed LUCI. A user who prefers crisp, professional exchanges gets exactly that.
- **Contextual memory** — LUCI connects the dots across conversations, so you don't have to re-explain context you've already shared.

## Personalization Timeline

LUCI begins adapting from your very first conversation, but the experience deepens meaningfully over the first week of regular use. Here's roughly what to expect:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Day 1 — Baseline">
    LUCI starts from a neutral, warm default. It's listening, noticing your tone and the kinds of things you ask about, and establishing a baseline for your communication style.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Days 3–5 — Tone Adjustments">
    LUCI begins reflecting your preferred tone and response length more consistently. Greetings start to feel more tailored, and LUCI may begin anticipating the type of help you're looking for.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Day 7+ — Proactive Suggestions">
    With a solid pattern established, LUCI starts making proactive suggestions — surfacing recurring topics, offering timely reminders, and shaping the conversation to fit your natural workflow.
  </Step>
</Steps>

The more regularly you use LUCI, the richer the adaptation becomes. Occasional use will still produce a personalized experience — it just develops more gradually.

## Staying in Control

Adaptive learning is meant to work quietly in the background, but you always have full visibility into what LUCI has picked up — and the ability to correct or clear it at any time.

**Viewing your learned preferences:**
Navigate to **Settings → Personalization → My Preferences** to see a summary of what LUCI currently understands about you. This includes your inferred tone preference, typical active hours, frequently discussed topics, and response length preference.

**Correcting a preference:**
If LUCI has gotten something wrong — maybe it thinks you prefer brief replies, but you actually enjoy detail — you can update any preference directly from the My Preferences screen. Tap the preference you want to change and select a new value, or let LUCI know in conversation (for example: "I actually prefer longer explanations").

**Resetting your learning profile:**
If you'd like to start fresh entirely, go to **Settings → Personalization → My Preferences → Reset Learning Profile**. LUCI will return to its defaults and begin observing again from scratch. This is permanent, so you'll be asked to confirm before anything is cleared.

<Note>
All personalization data lives securely in your LUCI account. You can export a copy of your preferences data or delete it entirely at any time from **Settings → Privacy → My Data**.
</Note>

<Tip>
The fastest way to shape LUCI's behavior is to tell it directly. Use the thumbs up / thumbs down on any response, or just say something like "I prefer shorter answers" or "Can you be a bit more formal?" — LUCI will update its understanding immediately and carry that forward.
</Tip>

Talking with LUCI for the first time feels a little different from using a search engine or a traditional chatbot — it's designed to be a real back-and-forth conversation, not a one-shot query. This page teaches you how to navigate the chat interface, what kinds of things to bring to LUCI, how to interpret its responses, and how to keep a conversation flowing naturally across multiple turns.

<Tip>
  There's no need to phrase things formally or carefully. LUCI adapts to your natural communication style — whether you're a bullet-point thinker, a stream-of-consciousness writer, or somewhere in between. Just say what's on your mind, the way you'd say it to a thoughtful friend.
</Tip>

## Starting a Chat

When you open LUCI, the main chat screen is your home base. The layout is intentionally minimal so you can focus on the conversation rather than the interface.

**The Input Bar**
At the bottom of the screen, you'll find the **input bar** — a soft-edged text field with the placeholder text *"What's on your mind?"* This is where every conversation begins.

- **To type a message**, tap or click the input bar to bring up your keyboard, write your message, and tap the **Send** button (the arrow icon on the right side of the bar) — or press **Enter** on a desktop keyboard.
- **To use your voice**, tap the **microphone icon** on the left side of the input bar. Speak naturally; LUCI transcribes your words in real time and displays them in the input bar before sending. Tap the microphone again to stop recording, or wait for a brief pause and it will send automatically.

You can send a message as short as a single word or as long as several paragraphs — LUCI handles both gracefully.

## What to Ask LUCI

LUCI is a versatile companion. It doesn't have a single fixed purpose — it's built to be genuinely helpful across many areas of life. Here are some example prompts to spark ideas across different categories:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Planning & Productivity" icon="calendar-check">
    *"Help me plan my week. I have a project deadline on Thursday, a dinner on Friday, and I've been putting off a hard conversation with a colleague."*
  </Card>
  <Card title="Emotional Support" icon="heart">
    *"I'm feeling stressed about a deadline at work and I can't seem to focus. Can we talk through it?"*
  </Card>
  <Card title="Learning & Explanation" icon="book-open">
    *"Explain quantum computing to me simply — I have a science background but I've never studied physics."*
  </Card>
  <Card title="Creative Projects" icon="pen-nib">
    *"Give me five ideas for a short story set in a city that never gets dark. I like quiet, atmospheric fiction."*

These are just starting points. You can also ask LUCI to help you draft an email, think through a difficult decision, summarize something you've read, practice a skill, or simply keep you company while you work. There's no topic that's off-limits — if LUCI isn't the right tool for something, it will tell you honestly.

## Reading LUCI's Responses

LUCI's responses are written in calm, clear prose — conversational but never flippant, warm but never over-the-top. Here's what to expect as you read a reply:

**Response Format**
LUCI writes in plain paragraphs by default, breaking into bullet points or numbered lists only when structure genuinely helps (like when outlining steps or comparing options). It doesn't pad responses with unnecessary disclaimers or filler phrases — if the answer is short, the response will be short.

**Clarifying Questions**
When your message is open-ended or could go several directions, LUCI may ask a clarifying question before diving in. For example, if you say *"Help me write something,"* LUCI might ask *"What kind of thing — an email, a story, a journal entry?"* This isn't a limitation; it's LUCI being thoughtful rather than guessing. You can answer the question or simply add more detail in your next message.

**The Typing Indicator**
While LUCI is preparing a response, you'll see a soft pulsing animation of three dots near the bottom of the conversation. This is the typing indicator — it means LUCI is actively generating a reply. Responses typically appear within one to three seconds, though longer or more complex requests may take a moment more.

## Continuing the Conversation

One of the most powerful things about LUCI is that it holds context throughout your session — you don't have to re-explain yourself with every message.

**Context Within a Session**
LUCI remembers everything you've said within the current conversation thread. If you mention early on that you're planning a trip to Lisbon and later ask *"What's the best time to arrive?"*, LUCI understands you're still talking about Lisbon. You can refer back to earlier parts of the conversation naturally, just as you would with another person.

**Starting a New Thread**
When you want a fresh start — maybe you've finished one topic and want to move on to something unrelated — tap the **New Conversation** button (the compose icon in the top-right corner of the chat screen). This opens a blank thread. Your previous conversation is saved and accessible in your history; starting a new thread simply means LUCI won't carry context from the old one.

**Scrolling Back Through History**
To revisit an older message in your current thread, scroll up in the chat window — the full history of the session is there. To find conversations from previous days, tap the **History** icon in the bottom navigation bar. Conversations are listed chronologically with an auto-generated title based on the topic. Tap any conversation to open it and pick up where you left off.

<Note>
  Your conversation history is saved automatically. When your device is online, history syncs to the cloud instantly so it's available on all your devices. When you're offline, conversations are saved locally and sync as soon as you reconnect — nothing is lost.
</Note>

LUCI's creative assistant isn't a content machine — it's a co-creative partner. There's a meaningful difference. Rather than generating polished output and handing it back to you as a finished product, LUCI works alongside your ideas, your voice, and your vision. It starts where you are, builds on what you bring, and helps you get from a blank page or a half-formed thought to something you're genuinely proud of. Whether you're writing a professional email, developing a story world, or just trying to get unstuck, LUCI meets you there.

## Writing Assistance

LUCI can help you write almost anything — emails, essays, journal entries, social media posts, cover letters, speech drafts, product descriptions, and more. The process is always collaborative: you tell LUCI what you need, it offers a first draft, and then you shape it together from there.

To get started, describe what you want to write and give LUCI enough context to work with: who it's for, what tone you're going for, and any key points you want to hit. The more you share, the better the first draft. From there, you can ask LUCI to adjust the tone, tighten the language, expand a section, or try a completely different approach.

Here are a few prompts to get you started:

```
Draft a professional email declining a meeting request while keeping the relationship warm.
```

```
Help me write a short bio for my portfolio — I'm a UX designer with five years of experience,
and I want it to sound human, not like a LinkedIn summary.
```

```
Rewrite this paragraph in a more casual tone — here it is: [paste your text]
```

You can paste your own writing directly into the conversation and ask LUCI to work with it. LUCI will treat your words as the foundation and build from there — it won't overwrite your voice, it'll help you refine it.

## Brainstorming

Sometimes the most valuable thing isn't a polished draft — it's a space to think freely and let ideas surface without filtering them too early. LUCI is built for that kind of open-ended creative thinking.

You can use LUCI for **mind-mapping conversations**: start with a topic or question and ask LUCI to help you branch out from it. *"I'm planning a workshop on resilience — what are some angles I haven't considered?"* LUCI will generate directions, ask questions that push you further, and help you see connections between ideas you might not have linked on your own.

When you want to generate a lot of ideas without self-editing, try **"Yes, and..." mode**. Just tell LUCI you want to brainstorm without judgment: *"I want to explore some wild ideas for my brand — don't filter, just build on whatever I throw out."* In this mode, LUCI responds expansively, treats every idea as a starting point rather than a conclusion, and adds momentum to your thinking rather than slowing it down with caveats. It's creative thinking at its most generative.

You can also ask LUCI to challenge your ideas once you've generated them — *"Now play devil's advocate on these"* — to stress-test your thinking before you commit to a direction.

## Story & Creative Prompts

If you write fiction, create games, develop worlds, or just love storytelling, LUCI is a versatile creative collaborator. It can generate story starters, develop characters, suggest plot twists, describe settings in vivid detail, and help you build out the rules and textures of an imagined world.

To generate a story prompt, tell LUCI what you're working with: the genre, the mood, any constraints you want to honor. The more specific you are, the more useful the prompt:

- *"Give me a mystery story opener set in a small coastal town, with an unreliable narrator."*
- *"I need a character concept: a reluctant mentor figure for a fantasy story with a non-Western setting."*
- *"Help me build out the magic system for my world — it should have a cost and feel grounded."*

You can also use LUCI to get unstuck mid-story. If you know where your story needs to go but can't figure out how to get there, describe the situation and ask LUCI to suggest three possible paths forward. Pick the one that resonates, then keep writing.

Set the tone for any creative session by telling LUCI the register you're working in — dark and literary, playful and whimsical, tense and sparse. LUCI will mirror that energy in everything it generates.

## Refining Your Work

Once you have a draft — yours, LUCI's, or some combination — the refining process is where things really come together. LUCI can give you feedback, offer alternatives, and help you strengthen your writing in specific, targeted ways.

Here are some of the ways you can ask LUCI to improve a piece of writing:

- **Feedback** — *"What's working in this draft and what isn't?"*
- **Alternative phrasings** — *"Give me three different ways to open this paragraph."*
- **Simplification** — *"This feels too dense — can you make it clearer without losing the meaning?"*
- **Expansion** — *"I want to say more about this point — can you help me develop it?"*
- **Tone adjustment** — *"This reads a bit cold — can you warm it up?"*

You can also use the **"Improve this"** shortcut: highlight any text in the LUCI editor and tap **Improve this** from the context menu. LUCI will offer a refined version and explain the changes it made, so you can decide what to keep.

<Tip>
  If a creative session is going well — a brainstorm that opened up something unexpected, or a draft you're excited about — save it as a **Note** before you close the conversation. Tap the bookmark icon in the top-right corner of any conversation to save it. You can find all your saved creative sessions in **Notes** in the main menu, ready to pick back up whenever inspiration strikes again.
</Tip>

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Can LUCI write in my personal style?">
    Yes — and it gets better at this over time. LUCI pays attention to the way you write: your sentence rhythm, the vocabulary you favor, the level of formality you're comfortable with. When you share your own writing and ask LUCI to continue or rewrite it, it uses that sample as a style guide.

    To give LUCI an explicit style reference, paste an example of your writing at the start of the session and say something like *"Here's a sample of how I write — please match this style."* You can also go to **Settings → Creative Assistant → My Writing Style** to save a style sample that LUCI will reference in all your creative sessions.

    The more you write with LUCI, the more naturally it mirrors your voice.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="How do I export what we created?">
    You can export any conversation, draft, or saved Note in a few formats. Open the conversation or Note you want to export, tap the **⋯** menu in the top-right corner, and select **Export**. From there, you can choose:

    - **Plain text (.txt)** — clean text with no formatting
    - **Markdown (.md)** — formatted text, great for use in writing apps or documentation tools
    - **Copy to clipboard** — paste directly into any app

    If you're working on a longer document across multiple sessions, save it as a Note and use the **Export** option from the Notes screen to pull it all together in one place.
  </Accordion>
